A Brief History



"In 2000 I designed and built my very first website with NetObjects Fusion, and that is where my passion for web developemnt started. I thought it was just a fun learning experience, but I had no idea what path this project would lead me down or how profound a change it would make in my life. As fate would have it, not long after completing my project, the company I was managing at the time had to revamp its website. I was immediately able to manage this entire project, based solely on what I had learned while completing my own website project.

Later, as Manager, Automotive Services for AAA Carolinas, I again used my web and database design experience and implemented various IT technology/solution platforms facilitating report/service automation such as: SQL database on UNIX OS, automated ERS service deployment utilizing Automated Vehicle Locating (AVL) technology, interactive website for AAA Carolinas branch offices and contracted provider network use. The SQL database was a 12-month project that I managed from conception to final implementation, fully automating the Emergency Road Service reporting. This transformed how AAA Carolinas monitored and controlled service deployment to its members, as well as positively affecting other areas of the business, and saving AAA Carolinas 3+ million dollars within the first year of implementation.

I yet again I found myself utilizing my web development and database skills while working for a marketing company. In my key account sales role, I was responsible for marketing campaign strategy development and overall project management for client projects. While in this role I sold, designed and managed the first B2B website project produced by the company. That's when I realized that I was not only drawn to web development but that it was where I was meant to be.

Today I am proud to say that I am a life-long learner with a passion for web development, formally trained in the UNC Charlotte Web Development program as a Full Stack Developer. I am very active in the coding community, which you can view under my Meetup Groups. I participate in training events as well as civic Hackathons to help others live a more fullfilling life through the use of technology."


~ David Blight
